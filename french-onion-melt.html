French Onion Melt

Slow-caramelised onions, melted Gruyère, and whole grain Dijon on golden GF sourdough. Like French onion soup in sandwich form — deeply savory and rich.

Prep time: 10 minutes

Cook time: 55 minutes

Total time: 65 minutes

Yield: 2 servings

Category: Sandwich

Cuisine: French

Keywords: gluten-free, vegetarian, French onion, Gruyère, caramelised onions, GF sourdough

Ingredients

  • 4 thick slices GF sourdough
  • 250g Gruyère, grated
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 2 tbsp whole grain Dijon mustard
  • 3 large yellow onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tsp caster sugar
  • 2 tbsp dry white wine or sherry (optional)
  • 1 tsp fresh thyme leaves
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 pinch freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

Caramelise the onions

Start the onions: Melt butter and olive oil in a wide, heavy-bottomed pan over medium-low heat. Add sliced onions and salt. Stir well to coat.

Caramelise low and slow: Cook over medium-low heat for 40–45 minutes, stirring every 5 minutes. They should turn deeply golden and jammy. Add the caster sugar halfway through to help them colour.

Deglaze and finish onions: Splash in the white wine or sherry if using, and stir until evaporated. Stir in fresh thyme and black pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning. Remove from heat.

Build and grill the sandwich

Butter the bread: Spread softened butter evenly on one side of each slice of GF sourdough. These buttered sides will face outward against the pan for a golden crust.

Build the sandwiches: Flip 2 slices butter-side down and spread Dijon mustard on the inner face. Pile the caramelised onions generously on top. Cover with a thick layer of grated Gruyère. Close with remaining bread, butter-side up.

Grill until golden: Heat a skillet or griddle over medium-low heat. Place sandwiches in the pan and press gently with a spatula. Cook until the underside is deeply golden, about 4–5 minutes.

Flip and melt: Carefully flip and cook the other side for another 3–4 minutes until golden and the Gruyère is visibly melted and bubbling at the edges.

Rest and serve: Remove from the pan and rest for 1 minute before slicing diagonally. Serve immediately with cornichons and a bitter leaf salad.
