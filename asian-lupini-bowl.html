Warm Asian-Inspired Lupini Bean & Grain Bowl

A hearty, protein-rich bowl featuring tender lupini beans, your choice of grain, vibrant greens, and a savory sesame-ginger dressing.

Yields: 2 servings
Prep time: 15 minutes
Cook time: 20-30 minutes
Total time: 45 minutes (approx.)

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked brown rice (approx. 1/2 cup uncooked) OR 4 oz (113g) dry soba noodles
  • 1 (15-oz / 425g) jar or can lupini beans, thoroughly drained and rinsed
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece fresh ginger, grated or minced
  • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce or tamari (for gluten-free)
  • 1 tbsp rice vinegar
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame oil
  • 1 tsp maple syrup or agave nectar (or a pinch of sugar)
  • 1/2 tsp sriracha or chili garlic sauce (optional, for heat)
  • 2 cups chopped bok choy (about 1 small head), OR 1.5 cups broccoli florets, OR 1.5 cups snow peas
  • 1 cup shredded carrots or matchstick carrots
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil (like canola, grapeseed, or avocado oil)
  • 1 tbsp toasted sesame seeds (for garnish)
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced (for garnish)
  • Red pepper flakes (optional, for garnish)

Instructions

  1. Cook the Grain:

    • For Brown Rice: Cook brown rice according to package directions. (Generally, 1 part rice to 2 parts water, bring to boil, reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes until water is absorbed and rice is tender.)
    • For Soba Noodles: Bring a pot of water to a rolling boil. Add soba noodles and cook according to package directions (usually 4-6 minutes) until al dente. Drain well and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking, then drain again thoroughly. Set aside.

  2. Prepare the Lupini Beans & Sauce:

    • In a small bowl, whisk together the soy sauce (or tamari), rice vinegar, toasted sesame oil, maple syrup (or agave), and sriracha (if using). Set aside.
    • Heat a large skillet or wok over medium heat. Add 1 tbsp neutral oil.
    • Add the minced garlic and grated ginger to the hot oil. Saut√© for 30-60 seconds until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
    • Add the drained and rinsed lupini beans to the skillet. Stir-fry for 2-3 minutes to lightly warm them through and allow them to absorb some of the garlic and ginger flavor.
    • Pour about half of the prepared sauce over the lupini beans. Stir to coat and cook for another minute. Remove from heat and set aside.

  3. Cook the Greens & Veggies:

    • Using the same skillet (no need to wash), add the chopped bok choy, broccoli florets, or snow peas. If using broccoli, you can add a tablespoon or two of water and cover the skillet briefly to steam it until tender-crisp (about 3-5 minutes).
    • Stir-fry the greens until they are tender-crisp and vibrant (about 3-5 minutes, depending on the vegetable). Add the shredded carrots during the last minute of cooking, stirring just until heated through.

  4. Assemble the Bowls:

    • Divide the cooked grain between two serving bowls.
    • Arrange the warmed lupini beans on one side of the grain.
    • Place the cooked greens and carrots on the other side.
    • Drizzle the remaining half of the prepared sauce over the entire bowl.

  5. Garnish and Serve:

    • Garnish with toasted sesame seeds, thinly sliced green onions, and a sprinkle of red pepper flakes if desired for extra heat.
    • Serve immediately and enjoy your protein-packed, flavorful meal!

Tips & Variations:

  • Extra Protein Boost: Add some edamame (shelled) along with your greens, or pan-fried tofu/tempeh cubes.
  • More Veggies: Feel free to add other quick-cooking vegetables like sliced mushrooms, bell peppers, or bean sprouts.
  • Nutty Crunch: Add a few chopped cashews or peanuts for extra texture.
  • Spice Level: Adjust the sriracha to your preference, or omit it entirely.
  • Meal Prep: Cook the grain and prepare the sauce ahead of time. Store components separately and assemble when ready to eat. You can gently reheat the beans and greens or enjoy the bowl at room temperature.
