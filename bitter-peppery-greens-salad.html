Bitter & Peppery Greens Salad

A sharp, refreshing French-style salad of frisée, radicchio, and endive with a punchy Dijon vinaigrette — the perfect counterpoint to a rich, cheesy French Onion Melt.

Prep time: 10 minutes

Cook time: 4 minutes

Total time: 14 minutes

Yield: 2 servings

Category: Salad

Cuisine: French

Keywords: gluten-free, vegan, vegetarian, bitter greens, frisée, radicchio, endive, Dijon vinaigrette

Ingredients

  • 60g frisée (curly endive), torn into bite-sized pieces
  • 40g radicchio, leaves torn
  • 1 Belgian endive (witloof), sliced crosswise
  • 3 tbsp walnuts, roughly chopped
  • 1 shallot, very finely minced
  • 2 tbsp fresh flat-leaf parsley leaves
  • 1 tsp whole grain Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 tsp smooth Dijon mustard
  • 1 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp honey
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 pinch freshly cracked black pepper

Instructions

Toast the walnuts: In a dry skillet over medium heat, toast the walnuts for 3–4 minutes, stirring often, until fragrant and lightly golden. Tip onto a plate to cool.

Make the vinaigrette: In a small bowl or jar, combine the minced shallot, whole grain Dijon, smooth Dijon, red wine vinegar, and honey. Whisk together, then slowly drizzle in the olive oil while whisking constantly until emulsified and slightly thickened. Season with salt and black pepper. The dressing should be quite sharp and bold.

Prepare the greens: Combine frisée, radicchio, and Belgian endive in a large bowl.

Dress and toss: Drizzle about two-thirds of the vinaigrette over the greens and toss gently but thoroughly. Add more dressing to taste — you want every leaf lightly coated.

Finish and serve: Scatter over the toasted walnuts and parsley leaves. Serve immediately — this salad wilts quickly once dressed.
