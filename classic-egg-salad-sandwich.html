Classic Egg Salad Sandwich

Creamy, comforting egg salad with Dijon mustard, celery, chives, and a hint of paprika — piled generously on crisp butter lettuce and gluten-free sourdough.

Prep time: 10 minutes

Cook time: 15 minutes

Total time: 55 minutes

Yield: 2 servings

Category: Sandwich

Cuisine: American

Keywords: gluten-free, vegetarian, egg salad, classic, GF sourdough

Ingredients

  • 5 large eggs
  • 3 tbsp mayonnaise
  • 1 tsp whole grain Dijon mustard
  • 2 celery stalks, finely diced
  • 2 tbsp fresh chives, finely snipped
  • 1 tsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1 pinch freshly cracked black pepper
  • 4 thick slices gluten-free sourdough
  • 4 butter lettuce leaves
  • 1 ripe tomato, thinly sliced (optional)

Instructions

Hard boil the eggs: Place the eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water by 2cm. Bring to a rolling boil over medium-high heat, then cover, remove from heat, and let sit for exactly 11 minutes.

Ice bath: Transfer eggs immediately to a bowl of ice water and leave for at least 5 minutes. This stops the cooking and makes peeling much easier.

Peel and chop: Peel the eggs and chop them to your preferred texture. A mix of roughly chopped and a couple of finely mashed eggs gives the best creamy-yet-textured result.

Mix the salad: In a bowl, combine the chopped eggs with mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, celery, chives, and lemon juice. Season with smoked paprika, salt, and black pepper. Fold gently — don't overmix. Taste and adjust seasoning.

Chill: Cover and refrigerate the egg salad for 20–30 minutes before serving. The flavours meld and it firms up nicely for easier spreading.

Build and serve: Lay butter lettuce on 2 slices of gluten-free sourdough. Pile on the egg salad generously. Add tomato slices if using. Top with the remaining bread, press gently, slice diagonally, and serve.
